ARCHIVE: How conformity is a lie and why I am happier as a nonconformist

11/15/1991
Chris McCandless

As I have gone through life, I have realised that I have been lied to. My parents have tried to make me like them and have made me believe that the only way to be happy in life is to conform to a standard. This includes but is not limited to having a stable job, having gone to college, and having a stable family. However, this approach to happiness is flawed and doesn't apply to everyone, including myself. I think conforming to standards is not a good way to lead a prosperous life, and should be more focused on being ourselves for once.

Throughout my life, I have lived in a very conformist family. They have made it a point to conform to the standard I have previously mentioned, and because of that, I was forced through college. I did pretty well in most of the things I did, but I didn't like the conformity involved. After I graduated, I decided to leave town, and live fully on my own. This led me to hitchhike across the American Southwest (and maybe even a small bit of Mexico), I have experienced a taste of nonconformity and just being myself. This has sparked some of my happier moments, and I feel it solidifies the fact that conformity is a complete lie.

Thanks to my experiences, I have experienced a taste of conformity and nonconformity
