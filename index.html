About Me

Career Summary and Goals

Throughout my life, I have always been involved in engineering work. I was part of my high school’s robotics team, where I developed software and designed parts in CAD. I also worked with a team to develop a device for cars that detected lights and sirens from emergency vehicles to make them more noticeable in vehicles with poor visibility and large amounts of sound deadening. I am currently studying Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Advanced Topics at Florida Polytechnic University with expectations to graduate in May 2027. Through Florida Poly, I have attended two Hackathon-style events in which I worked on teams to construct various devices, such as a robotic arm and an automatic Etch-A-Sketch. I have been certified in the Autodesk Inventor CAD software, have experience in various programming languages, like C/C++, and Python, and am currently learning Solidworks in the hopes of getting certified. I also have experience with the KiCad EDA suite, and experience with popular micro-controller development boards and single board computers, such as Arduino, ESP32, and Raspberry Pi. I am a go-getter who takes pride in my work and can effectively manage my time, and can get work done on time.

I am seeking an internship in Electrical Engineering in the hopes that I can gain further experience in the field. I also hope to use the experience from these internships to obtain connections within the industry and make it into an entry-level position to start my career.
